The Yellowstone Hotspot is a 16 million year old volcano that currently sits beneath Yellowstone National Park. In the last 2.1 million years, there have been 3 major eruptions, forming three calderas, most recent being the Yellowstone caldera formed 640,000 years ago. These eruptions helped to create the topography of the western United States, and contributes to Yellowstone's incredible scenery, geology and hydrothermal features.Within the area of Yellowstone, there are approximately 700 to 3,000 earthquakes each year, most of which cannot be felt. This is because of the extensive network of faults associated with the Yellowstone volcano and the surrounding tectonic features.

The Yellowstone Caldera is outlined in red. This is the most recently formed caldera, others have been omitted. The Sour Creek and Mallard Lake Resurgent domes are outlined in green. These domes form when magma rises to the shallow levels beneath a caldera and slowly reinflate the previously deflated magma resevoir, pushing the ground upwards to form a dome.
